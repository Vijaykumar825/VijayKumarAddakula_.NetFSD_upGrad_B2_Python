The Mustang GT is not just a sports car — it’s a statement. Powered by a naturally aspirated 5.0-liter Coyote V8, it pushes out around 450–460 horsepower depending on the model year, delivering raw, linear power without turbo lag.
The throttle response is sharp, the exhaust note is aggressive, and the rear-wheel-drive layout gives you proper driver engagement. It comes with either a 6-speed manual for purists or a 10-speed automatic for faster shifts and better acceleration.
Inside, you get a driver-focused cockpit with a digital instrument cluster, performance modes, and track apps that let you monitor real-time metrics.
The Mustang GT isn’t built to be subtle — it’s built to be loud, muscular, and emotionally addictive. If you want efficiency, look elsewhere. If you want presence, power, and old-school V8 dominance in a modern chassis, this is it.
